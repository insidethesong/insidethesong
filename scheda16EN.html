ME SO 'MRIACATO

"Me so' mbriacato" is a song by the Italian singer-songwriter Mannarino.
The title "Me so' mbriacato" is a Roman dialect expression that means "I got drunk" in Italian. The term "mbriacato" can be interpreted both literally, referring to alcohol intoxication, and figuratively, indicating a sort of ecstasy or abandonment of oneself.
The lyrics of the song are poetic and rich in imagery, evoking a sense of freedom, joy, and rebirth. The song expresses a sense of escape from everyday worries through the intoxication of love and music. The track invites the listener to be carried away by the strength and beauty of life.
"Me so' mbriacato" has become one of Mannarino's most representative songs and an anthem to the freedom of life and love for music. Its combination of original sounds, poetic lyrics, and engaging performance has made this song a landmark in the artist's discography.

MANNARINO

Mannarino, whose real name is Alessandro Mannarino, is an Italian singer-songwriter born on August 23, 1978, in Rome. From a young age, Mannarino developed a deep love for music and started playing the guitar and composing his first songs.
His music career took off in the 2000s when he began performing in venues and clubs in Rome. His unique and original musical style, which blends Italian popular sounds with folk and world music influences, captured the attention of both the audience and critics.
In 2009, Mannarino released his first album titled "Bar della Rabbia," which received great public acclaim and helped establish his talent as a singer-songwriter. The album featured songs like "Scendi giù" and "Me so' mbriacato," which became true hits.
Mannarino is also known for his intense live performances, where he manages to engage the audience with his energy and passion for music. His shows are a mix of captivating sounds, profound lyrics, and a strong stage presence.
