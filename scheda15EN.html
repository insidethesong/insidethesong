I WILL ALWAYS LOVE YOU

The song was originally written and performed by Dolly Parton in 1973. It became one of the most iconic songs in pop music thanks to Whitney Houston's rendition in 1992 for the movie "The Bodyguard".
Dolly Parton wrote "I Will Always Love You" as a farewell message to Porter Wagoner, her musical partner whom she had worked with for years. The song reflects feelings of gratitude, respect, and love as Parton embarked on her solo career.
"I Will Always Love You" reached the top of the charts in the United States on two separate occasions, once with Dolly Parton's rendition in 1974 and again with Whitney Houston's version in 1992. It was the first song in history to reach the top of the charts twice with the same performer.
Whitney Houston's powerful interpretation of the song, with her emotional vocal crescendo in the chorus, became a landmark for female voices in the music world. It remained at the number one spot on the Billboard Hot 100 for 14 consecutive weeks, making it one of the most successful songs of all time.

DOLLY PARTON

Dolly Parton, born in 1946 in Locust Ridge, Tennessee, is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. She is one of the most influential artists in the history of country music and has gained widespread popularity internationally.
Parton grew up in a modest family and started singing at a young age.
She was discovered by Porter Wagoner, a country music legend, who invited her to become one of his backup singers and musical partners. This collaboration launched Parton's career and enabled her to achieve commercial success.
Throughout her career, Dolly Parton has released numerous successful albums and recorded a series of impactful singles. Some of her most famous songs include "Jolene," "9 to 5," and "I Will Always Love You." Her unique voice, accompanied by her stage charisma, has captivated audiences worldwide.
In addition to her music career, Parton has also found success as an actress, appearing in various films and television series. She has been nominated for Emmy Awards for her performances in "Dolly Parton's Christmas of Many Colors: Circle of Love" and "Dolly Parton's Heartstrings."
With a career spanning over five decades, Dolly Parton is considered an icon of country music and one of the most beloved artists in the world.
